## Reading the layered specs

The naive correlation is $\hat\beta_{M0} = -0.262$ — non-metro projects
have leverage about 0.262× lower than metro on average, in raw means.

Adding year FE (M1) shifts the coefficient by 0.013 —
a small amount,
confirming that origination-year composition is not what drives the gap.

Adding QALICB-type FE (M2) shrinks
the estimate to -0.186*** — the "rural projects are more NRE-skewed" composition story
explains 24% of the M0 gap.

Adding state FE (M3) further shrinks
the estimate at -0.172**.

**Adding CDE FE (M4 — the workhorse) reduces $|\hat\beta|$ by 82.2%
relative to M0**, to -0.047 (clustered SE 0.101). This is the
key decomposition number: roughly 82%
of the raw rural penalty is explained by *which CDEs* deploy rural deals (between-CDE
selection), and the remaining ~18%
is the *within-CDE* rural penalty — the same intermediary, the same year, the same
QALICB type, deploying the same federal credit, mobilizes systematically less private
capital when it goes rural.

The median spec (M4-Q, -0.001) confirms the OLS finding is not a tail story.

## Interaction (M5)

The M5 specification interacts the rural indicator with QALICB-type. Coefficients in
`main_table.md` show whether the rural penalty differs by project type. The
hypothesis from Figure 5 is that the gap is largest in RE (real estate, where private
debt-stacking is most natural) and smallest in CDE (loan-to-CDE, where leverage is
mechanically pinned at 1×).

## Bunching diagnostic — the 20% mandate

Of 310 CDEs with ≥ 5 QLICI transactions, the cross-CDE
distribution of non-metro deal shares shows excess mass at the 20% statutory line of
**-2.2%** above a 5th-order polynomial counterfactual fit
on the surrounding distribution.

**Verdict: no clean evidence of binding bunching at the 20% line**
in the data. This is a moderately surprising finding worth investigating. The mandate may bind only at the CDE-allocation-round level (not the cumulative-deployment level), or compliance may be tracked at a different aggregation.

See `figures/6_bunching_diagnostic.png` for the visual.
