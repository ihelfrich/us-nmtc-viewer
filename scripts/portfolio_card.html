NMTC Blended-Finance Atlas — Phase 0

Public Finance Spatial Econometrics Regression Discontinuity CesiumJS Python

Author: Dr. Ian Helfrich

Research Question: Does the US federal New Markets Tax Credit mobilize private capital differently in rural vs. urban markets — and is the rural leverage gap a story about market structure or about which intermediary CDEs get allocated to rural deals?

Setting & identification:

  • Data: CDFI Fund NMTC Public Data Release FY2003–FY2022 — 19,907 QLICI transactions across 8,024 projects, $66.6 B of public credit deployed, $120.9 B of total project cost
  • Within-program FE decomposition: CDE × year × QALICB-type fixed effects to decompose the rural leverage gap into between-CDE selection vs. within-CDE deployment heterogeneity
  • LIC-eligibility RDD: sharp 20%-poverty cutoff (or 80%-MFI) as a fuzzy first-stage instrument for NMTC investment, run separately for metro and non-metro to identify the rural × treatment-effect interaction
  • 20% mandate bunching test: Chetty–Kleven excess-mass estimator on the cross-CDE distribution of non-metro deal share, validating that the statutory minimum binds for a subset of intermediaries

Headline empirics: Median metro leverage 1.19×, non-metro 1.07×; mobilization ratio 0.82× across the program. The within-CDE rural penalty is statistically zero (β = −0.05, p = 0.64) — about 80% of the aggregate rural gap is between-CDE selection, not market structure. The mechanism candidate.

Technical Implementation: Browser-based presentation: CesiumJS 1.118 over tokenless Esri World Imagery with all 8,019 geocoded projects rendered as filterable Cartesian primitives, year-range slider, KaTeX-rendered econometric specifications, fully reproducible Python pipeline (scripts/describe_nmtc.py, scripts/run_regressions.py, scripts/build_viewer_data.py), static GitHub Pages deploy with SHA-256-verified raw data alongside.

Positioning: Phase 0 of the proposed Spatial Public-Finance Atlas — a multi-program, geographically-disaggregated panel of US and international subsidy programs (NMTC, LIHTC, Opportunity Zones, IRA energy credits, MDB project lending) with their private-capital follow-on, in the spirit of the Opportunity Atlas but for the public-private finance interaction.
